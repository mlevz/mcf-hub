Call Openers — Cold, Warm & HELOC
How to open every type of call
Openers
▼
Tone First — Always

Talk slower (150–170 wpm). Talk louder than feels natural. Use pauses — silence is pressure. Mirror the client's pace and energy.

Cold Outbound
"Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] calling from Motor City Funding — business financing brokerage out of Detroit. Reason I'm reaching out is [business name] may qualify for a financing program we have — can I grab 60 seconds?"

If they say yes — stop talking, start qualifying immediately.

Inbound / Warm Lead
"Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] from Motor City Funding — you reached out about financing options for your business. I wanted to jump on while you're fresh — can I ask a couple quick questions to see what you qualify for?"

Call within 5 minutes. Every minute you wait, they cool off.

HELOC Opener — Use Whenever Property Comes Up
"One more quick question — do you own your home or any other real estate? Because we have a program that lets business owners pull from their home equity at a fraction of the rate of most business products. A lot of our clients use it for working capital, equipment, or paying off higher-rate debt. Is that worth 60 seconds?"
💡 Option-Based Questions: Never ask "do you want to move forward?" Ask "would you rather send the application first or the bank statements?" Give two doors both marked FORWARD.

The 12 Qualifying Questions
Work conversationally — every answer feeds your product match
Discovery
▼
1Are you getting calls from MCA brokers pushing high-interest short-term advances?Differentiates MCF; opens conversation about past experience.
2Do you have a line of credit?Checks for existing revolving credit — enough? Room for more?
3Do you have any current loans on the business?Identifies MCAs, term loans, SBA — feeds debt schedule conversation.
4Are you interested in debt consolidation?Opens reverse consolidation if they have multiple MCA positions.
5Have you ever borrowed with the SBA?If yes: experience? If no: sets up SBA pitch.
6How much did you gross last year?Core revenue qualifier. Annual ÷ 12 = monthly baseline.
7What is your FICO score?Primary routing question. 675+ → SBA. 650+ → SBA 504/CRE. 625+ → LOC. 620+ → HELOC. Below 620 → Credit Servicing.
8Do you own any residential or commercial property?Unlocks HELOC, DSCR, CRE. Follow-up: value, mortgage balance, rented?
9Do you have any plans for expansion or a major capital need?Surfaces use of funds and creates urgency.
10Have you filed your 2024–2025 tax returns?Needed for SBA, HELOC, CRE. Flags timeline issue if not filed.
11Do you accept credit cards?Identifies MCA merchant processing eligibility and revenue stream.
12Do you plan on purchasing equipment or real estate?Opens equipment financing, SBA 504, or CRE. Ask about invoice amount and purpose.

Tiered Close System
Always aim for Tier 1. Never stop below Tier 3.
Close

Application + Last 4 Months Bank Statements (+ Debt Schedule if applicable). Push for this every call.
"Based on what you've told me you look like a strong [PRODUCT] candidate. I need your application and the last 4 months of bank statements. Can you send those in the next hour?"
📄

Application OR bank statements. Either one is forward motion.
"Which is easier right now — knocking out the application or pulling the last 4 months of statements?"
📅

Specific date and time locked in. Text immediately + follow-up email with unique subject line, app link, Calendly link.
Text: "Hey [First Name], [Your Name] from Motor City Funding — text me back so you've got my number saved."
🚫 NON-NEGOTIABLE: You do not end a call without a next step. Application, bank statements, or a locked calendar invite. Every. Single. Call.

Objection Handling Guide
Acknowledge → Address → Ask. Stay in the loop.
Objections
▼

Every objection = a request for more confidence or information. Respond with positive tone and a follow-up question. Agree and redirect — never argue.

"I've dealt with guys like you before." ▾
Acknowledge immediately. "I completely understand — there are a lot of brokers pushing high-rate cash advances. That's not what we do." Differentiate: MCF offers SBA, HELOCs, LOCs, CRE — not just MCAs. Ask: "Have you ever borrowed through the SBA or gotten a line of credit?" — resets the conversation. Skeptical merchants who get comfortable convert at high rates.
"Now isn't a good time." ▾
Ask what specifically makes now bad timing. "Getting the docs ready doesn't commit you to anything — it just keeps the option open." If they still won't move — BAMFAM.
"The rates are too high." ▾
Ask what rate would work for them. Connect rate to context: what does the capital enable? What does inaction cost? If comparing to a bank: "How long have you been waiting on the bank?"
"I need to think about it." ▾
Ask what specifically they want to think through — name the concern, address it. "Sending the docs doesn't commit you to anything." Still won't move — BAMFAM.
"Send me more information." ▾
Offer to walk through key points now — it'll make the email more useful. If they insist: send it, then immediately BAMFAM. Email = bridge to next call, not the close. Unique subject line. One product, three bullet points, one CTA.
"How do I know this is legit?" ▾
Take it seriously. Explain MCF briefly: what we do, how we get paid (when they fund), no commitment from submitting an application. Offer references. Slower here is faster overall.
"I don't think I'll get approved." ▾
Ask what the concern is — credit, TIB, revenue? If credit: "We offer credit servicing that can clean that up and get you into better products faster." Turn the objection into discovery. Never let them self-select out without one clarifying question.
"We already have financing." ▾
Ask what they have and how it's working — you may find a gap. If multiple MCAs: reverse consolidation. If genuinely happy: note their info, flag for future call.
🔄 The Loop: Round 1 — Acknowledge + ask what's behind it. Round 2 — Acknowledge + reframe. Round 3 — Acknowledge + BAMFAM. Top reps handle 10–15 objections on one call without losing composure.

BAMFAM — Book A Meeting From A Meeting
Standard for every call — including the ones you close
BAMFAM
▼
  • Confirm next action before hanging up — specific date and time, not "sometime next week"
  • Send Calendly invite immediately after the call
  • Text: "Hey [First Name], [Your Name] from Motor City Funding — text me back so you've got my number saved."
  • Follow-up email with UNIQUE subject line specific to their situation (never "Follow Up")
  • Include app link + Calendly link in every follow-up email
BAMFAM Confirmation Text
"Hey [First Name], just confirming our call [Day] at [Time] — looking forward to it. [Your Name], Motor City Funding."
A deal isn't done until it funds. Between the application and money hitting their account, things happen. Your job is to stay in the deal.

Motor City Funding connects small business owners to capital — fast, through the right products, with a team that actually fights for their file. We work across MCAs, SBA loans, equipment financing, HELOCs, DSCR loans, CRE, and more.

Hunt Mode — Keeping Your Pipeline Full
Call notes, priority system, outbound protocol, BAMFAM

The Rule

You can't kill what you haven't hunted. Hunting is a system — not random dialing.

#1 — Call Notes (Non-Negotiable)

Every prospect gets a note after every touchpoint. A note has four elements: what you said or sent; what they said back; where they are in the process (first contact / qualified / docs requested / docs received / submitted); your next action and the date it should happen. If it isn't in the notes, it didn't happen.

#2 — Work Your List (Priority Order)

  • Prospects with a confirmed BAMFAM call today
  • Prospects who submitted partial docs — follow up to complete
  • Warm prospects who haven't moved — they need a nudge
  • Fresh outbound leads from today's list
  • Cold outbound — new contacts

Never skip to #4 or #5 when #1, #2, or #3 have open items.

#3 — Outbound Protocol

Your outbound call has one job: get them into a qualifying conversation. Introduction → qualifying questions → product match → close for docs. No wasted time re-explaining MCF before they've engaged.

#4 — Inbound Warm Lead Protocol

Call within 5 minutes of the lead coming in. Open with context — they responded to outreach about business financing. Move directly to qualifying. Speed is the edge: the rep who calls first wins most of the time.

#5 — End of Day Review (6:30 PM)

  • All call notes updated and complete
  • Every BAMFAM confirmed — prospect has call on calendar
  • All partial-doc prospects followed up
  • Tomorrow's priority list built and ranked
  • Submitted files checked for status updates
If Daily Review takes more than 15 minutes, you did not document during the day. Fix that tomorrow.

Kill Mode — From Qualified Prospect to Docs in Hand
Tone → Intro → Discovery → Match → Close → Objections → BAMFAM

Kill Mode is a sequence, not an improvisation. In that order. Every time.

Tone

Talk slower than you think you should (150–170 wpm). Talk louder than feels natural. Use pauses as a tool — after you ask an important question, stop talking. Mirror the client's pace, energy, and vocabulary.

Own the Conversation

There are no scripts in this office — scripts create a ceiling. A rep who actually understands the process can handle anything. Build that understanding, not a script. Use option-based questions: never ask "do you want fries?" Ask "small, medium, or large?"

Discovery — The Qualifying Questions

Work through the 12 qualifying questions conversationally. Every answer feeds your product match. You're answering one question: what does this business need, and do we have a product for it?

The Match

FICO first. Then revenue. Then property. Then TIB. Run the Quick Match Guide (Home page) to route instantly. When they qualify: "Based on what you've told me, you look like a strong [PRODUCT] candidate — this is [one-sentence why it fits]. Let's get your application started today."

After They Engage: Move Directly to Discovery

Do not add more context, re-explain MCF, or fill the silence. They're listening. Start qualifying.

Reading Financial Documents — Appendix C
Bank statements, Schedule C, Form 1120S — what to find and why it matters

Business Bank Statement — Key Numbers

  • Total Deposits: Sum of all money IN during the period. This is effective monthly revenue — most MCA products offer 50–150% of one month's deposits.
  • Number of Deposits: 8–15/month = steady cash flow. 1–2 large deposits = project-based/seasonal = higher risk. Some lenders require 5+ deposits/month minimum.
  • Average Daily Balance (ADB): Shows if the business actually keeps money. ADB should be at least 15–20% of avg monthly deposits. Low ADB = struggles with daily MCA remittances.
  • NSF / Returned Items: 0–1 = acceptable. 2–3 = caution. 4+ = most lenders decline. Always disclose NSF count upfront — hiding them causes post-submission declines.

Schedule C (Sole Proprietor) — Key Lines

  • Line 1 — Gross Receipts: Top-line revenue. Cross-reference against bank deposits. Large gaps = income in another account (ask) or inflated tax return (flag).
  • Line 7 — Gross Income: Revenue after COGS — shows gross margin efficiency.
  • Line 31 — Net Profit: What the owner actually earned after all expenses. Drives personal income for HELOC DTI calculations. Two consecutive losses = flag.

Form 1120S (S-Corporation) — Key Lines

  • Line 1a — Gross Receipts: Cross-reference against bank deposits.
  • Line 21 — Ordinary Business Income: Net profit that passes through to owners via K-1.
  • Line 7 — Officer Compensation: Owner salary (W-2). Total owner income = Officer W-2 + K-1 ordinary income.
  • Schedule K-1 Ownership %: All owners 20%+ must sign as personal guarantors. Always ask: "How is ownership split?" Get all owners above 20% to sign upfront.
You are not the underwriter — but you ARE the first filter. A rep who can quickly spot a strong file (high deposits, healthy ADB, low NSFs, clean tax returns) saves everyone time and builds lender credibility.

▼

Designated live-transfer leads from a source such as CJ or KD. Live Transfer is not a source prefix by itself — it is a routing override layered on top of a lead's underlying source.

Operational Meaning

Temporary assignment lane. These rows stay with designated reps for the configured cycle count before returning to their underlying source pool. The override expires after the configured number of cycles.

📐 Operational Rules
  • Source prefix ≠ sheet tab. Red Beetle and Green Beetle display on their own tabs but remain KD/Blue Beetle-family rows. Never assume the tab name equals the source prefix.
  • Active and App-in retained rows return to their current rep independent of ordinary scrub-fresh pool rules.
  • Temporary campaign restrictions should live on the lead/route metadata — similar to live-transfer behavior — not on the rep's account globally.
  • Scrubbed imports and reserve exports should not commit raw lead CSVs to version control. Source filenames and provenance are recorded in the Source Report only.
  • Retired or corrected labels (e.g. "Boiler Room" → "Previous Subs") remain auditable in history. Active rep-facing routing uses the current canonical tab names only.
